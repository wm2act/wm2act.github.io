TL;DR

World2Act aligns action and video in a shared latent space, then post-trains a residual policy using transferred world-model dynamics. A skill-compositional data pipeline makes arbitrary-length rollouts stable and improves both simulation and real-world control.

Abstract

World models can now imagine rich robotic futures, but existing WM-to-policy transfer often relies on pseudo-actions, pixel-space rewards, or heavy joint video-action modeling. World2Act introduces direct latent-to-policy alignment: a post-training recipe that aligns denoised world-model video latents with VLA action latents and transfers that dynamics prior into a residual policy. To handle long-horizon behavior, the framework decomposes instructions into atomic skills and aligns skill-level sub-videos chronologically. Across RoboCasa, LIBERO, and real-world manipulation, the method consistently improves over strong WM-based post-training baselines.

World models can dream robot behavior, but reliable control still lags behind

Core Research Question

How do we convert imagined dynamics into actionable control?

The slide deck motivates World2Act with a simple observation: modern world models can synthesize meaningful robot futures for tasks like picking a hotdog from a cabinet or pressing a coffee machine button.

  • World models already capture strong dynamics priors for robot interaction.
  • Direct transfer to VLA control is still brittle when supervision lives only in image space.
  • World2Act focuses on the missing bridge: latent action alignment between imagined video and executable action space.

Challenge

Pixel-space supervision alone is not enough for reliable robotic control

The failure examples in the slide deck highlight hallucinations and unstable rollouts when control improvement is driven only by image similarity.

Limitation

Hallucinations break the training signal

The challenge slide asks the key question directly: is pixel-space supervision enough for reliable control? World2Act answers no, and moves the alignment target into a shared latent space between imagined video and action.

  • Image-space losses can overfit appearance instead of behavior.
  • Small rollout errors compound across longer horizons.
  • Reliable policy correction needs a representation-level bridge instead of a rendered-frame surrogate.

Method

Direct latent-to-policy alignment is the core of World2Act

Instead of relying on pseudo-labels or rendered rewards, the method aligns world-model video latents with VLA action latents and transfers those dynamics into a residual policy.

World2Act

Two-stage transfer from imagined video to executable action

The method slides describe a shared latent space where world-model representations and action representations meet. World2Act first aligns these spaces, then post-trains a residual policy that uses the transferred dynamics prior during control.

  • Stage 1 aligns video latents and action latents with contrastive alignment plus action reconstruction.
  • Stage 2 post-trains a residual policy so the base VLA can absorb world-model dynamics without replacing the original controller.
  • The residual structure keeps the recipe modular and compatible with strong existing VLAs.

Key Contributions

What the homepage should leave readers with

  • Latent action alignment: world-model video dynamics are aligned directly with VLA action space instead of relying on rendered rewards.
  • Automatic skill pipeline: long-horizon behavior is decomposed into atomic subgoals with aligned skill videos for more stable generation and control.
  • Scalable post-training: the residual policy structure makes it practical to enhance strong base VLAs rather than train new monolithic controllers.
  • Simulation to real robot transfer: the results span RoboCasa, LIBERO, and physical Franka evaluation.
